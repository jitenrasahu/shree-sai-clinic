Understanding Surgical Treatments & Wisdom Tooth Extraction

Home Blog Wisdom Tooth Extraction

Wisdom teeth are the last set of molars that usually appear between the ages of 17 and 25. In many cases, these teeth grow normally without causing any problems. However, for some people, wisdom teeth may grow in the wrong direction, remain trapped inside the gums, or create pressure on nearby teeth. This condition can lead to severe pain, swelling, infection, difficulty in chewing, and jaw discomfort.

At Shree Sai Dental Clinic, Boudh, we provide safe, advanced, and painless surgical treatments for impacted or damaged wisdom teeth under the expert care of Dr. Bhagyaban Meher (Dental Surgeon).

Common Signs You May Need Extraction

  • Pain or swelling in the back side of the mouth.
  • Difficulty opening the mouth properly.
  • Gum infection or bleeding around the wisdom tooth.
  • Food getting stuck near the back molars.
  • Bad breath or unpleasant taste in the mouth.
  • Pressure or crowding of nearby teeth.

Why Wisdom Tooth Extraction Is Important

Ignoring wisdom tooth problems can lead to several complications, including:

  • Tooth decay in nearby teeth.
  • Gum disease and severe infections.
  • Jaw pain and chronic swelling.
  • Cyst formation around the impacted tooth.
  • Difficulty eating and speaking comfortably.

Early treatment helps prevent these complications and protects your overall oral health.

Our Surgical Treatment Procedure

3

Safe Tooth Removal

The impacted wisdom tooth is carefully and precisely removed using advanced surgical methods.

4

Post-Treatment Care

Patients receive proper guidance for swift healing, along with prescribed medicines and follow-up care.

Is the Procedure Painful?

Most patients fear wisdom tooth extraction, but with modern anesthesia and gentle surgical care, the procedure is usually highly comfortable and stress-free. Mild swelling or discomfort after treatment is completely normal and usually heals within just a few days.

Tips After Wisdom Tooth Surgery

  • Take prescribed medicines on time.
  • Avoid very hot or spicy foods for 24 hours.
  • Do not smoke or use tobacco.
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling.
  • Maintain proper, gentle oral hygiene.
